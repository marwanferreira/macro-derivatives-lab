Initial trade thesis:
This trade was selected as the strongest candidate from the pre-trade research process. Among the five strategies tested, the WTI call spread showed the best combination of macro clarity, pricing attractiveness, and execution feasibility. The core thesis was that oil remained the clearest live macro theme, with geopolitical and supply-related factors supporting upside risk in WTI. A bull call spread was preferred over outright futures or a naked long call because it provided a defined-risk bullish expression with a realistic breakeven and controlled downside.

What went wrong:
The main issue was not the initial trade idea. The strategy thesis and structure selection were reasonable. The main problem was the divergence between unrealized marked PnL and executable realized PnL. The simulator displayed very strong unrealized gains at one point, but those gains were not actually locked in when the spread was exited.

The key execution problem was:
- option spread marks on the platform were misleading
- the unrealized PnL overstated what could be captured in practice
- the exit was effectively realized leg-by-leg in a way that introduced severe slippage
- the short call re-priced heavily against the position at exit, offsetting the gain on the long call

Main lesson learned:
For multi-leg option strategies, especially in a simulator with noisy marks and delayed quotes, unrealized spread PnL should not be treated as realizable profit. The actual executable net value of the spread matters much more than marked profit shown on the screen. In future trades, exits should be managed using actual bid/ask checks and, where possible, limit-based spread exits rather than blindly flattening after seeing a favorable unrealized PnL.

Final conclusion:
The trade thesis and initial structure selection were reasonable, but the realized outcome differed sharply from peak unrealized PnL because the simulator’s option pricing and leg-by-leg exit execution introduced significant slippage. This made the trade a useful case study in execution risk, spread management, and the difference between theoretical and realizable profit.
